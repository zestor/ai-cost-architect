Roles & RACI

Every artifact names an owner. Every engagement names five signatories. These are the six roles the framework relies on — and what each one is accountable for.

The five signatory roles

An engagement cannot start until these five roles are named in Artifact 1. If a role is unfilled, the engagement is not ready.

RoleAccountability
Product OwnerOwns scope, priorities, and user-experience trade-offs. Signs Artifact 1 scope and quality gates in Artifact 16.
Business OwnerOwns the business case and the attributable-benefits claim. Signs Artifact 11 baseline and Artifact 13 exec one-pager.
Finance LeadOwns pricing table, forecast reconciliation, ROI/NPV model, and monthly close. Signs Artifacts 3, 8, 12.
Technical LeadOwns architecture decomposition, instrumentation, and cost-driver mapping. Signs Artifacts 2, 4, 5, 20.
Risk / Compliance LeadOwns model risk, third-party risk, and regulatory boundary. Signs Artifacts 15, 16 and MRM signoff where SR 11-7 applies.
Principal ConsultantOwns the framework itself — orchestrates the five owners, writes the narrative, drives cadence. Signs all artifacts after review.

RACI in practice

The Cost Management Operating Model (Artifact 17) defines the full RACI matrix, meeting cadence, and escalation path. Standing responsibility patterns:

  • Cost anomalies — Platform on-call responsible; Finance and Product informed within 30 minutes.
  • Model changes — Technical Lead responsible; Risk consulted; Product informed.
  • Pricing renegotiation — Finance responsible; Procurement consulted; Product informed.
  • Quality gate changes — Product responsible; Risk consulted; Technical informed.
