Governance Cadence

Once the operating model (Artifact 17) is live, cost management runs on a fixed cadence. Each rhythm has a named owner, a fixed artifact, and a specific outcome.

CadenceOwnerFocusArtifact
DailyPlatform on-callCost-anomaly dashboard review; run Artifact 21 if > 20% deviation→
WeeklyProduct + FinanceUnit-economics review; adoption trends; guardrail-trigger and reject rates→
MonthlyFinance (lead) + Product + TechnicalClose, reconcile Artifact 8 vs. invoice, publish variance memo→
QuarterlyLOB leadership + cost management triadPortfolio review; refresh optimization catalog; renegotiation checkpoints→
AnnualExecutive committeeFramework refresh; assumption re-baseline; portfolio prioritization→

Why cadence matters more than tools

The most common failure mode in enterprise AI cost management is not missing dashboards or missing data. It is missing rhythm — decisions that should be monthly happen quarterly, quarterly reviews slide to annual, and by year-end the forecast is a fantasy. The cadence above is the smallest set of recurring meetings that keeps the framework alive without the consultant present.

What to bring to each meeting

  • Daily — the anomaly dashboard, previous 24-hour deltas, any triggered playbook.
  • Weekly — cost-per-outcome trend, top 3 requests by volume, top 3 by cost.
  • Monthly — Artifact 8 vs. invoice reconciliation, variance memo, updated forecast.
  • Quarterly — refreshed Artifact 10 (optimization catalog), vendor renewals list.
  • Annual — Artifact 3 (pricing) refresh, Artifact 12 (ROI) recompute, Artifact 17 (operating model) audit.
