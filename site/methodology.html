The Methodology

A start-to-finish playbook for the Principal AI Solutions Consultant leading enterprise AI cost management engagements. Seven phases from portfolio intake through steady-state operate.

Read this in order

The methodology is designed to be read sequentially the first time and referenced by phase thereafter. Full narrative, worked examples, and cross-phase dependencies are in docs/METHODOLOGY.md in the repository. This page is the quick-navigation summary.

The seven phases

Phase 0 · Portfolio Intake

Timeline: Pre-week

Purpose: Decide whether to take the project and at what tier.

Exit criteria: Project accepted with tier assigned. Five named owners. Sponsor confirmed for kickoff.

Phase 1 · Engagement Kickoff

Timeline: Week 0–1

Purpose: Establish a signed, shared scope with named owners.

Exit criteria: Artifact 1 has zero blank fields. All five owners have signed. Decision log has ≥ 3 entries.

Primary artifacts:

  • Artifact 01 — Engagement Kickoff & Scope (Product)

Phase 2 · Decomposition & Boundary

Timeline: Week 1–2

Purpose: Turn architecture into measurable steps with a defensible cost boundary.

Exit criteria: Artifacts 2, 3, 4 approved. Drivers map 1:1 to instrumentation plan.

Primary artifacts:

  • Artifact 02 — Workload Decomposition & Architecture Notes (Technical)
  • Artifact 03 — Cost Boundary, Assumptions & Pricing Table (Finance)
  • Artifact 04 — Cost Driver Map (Technical)

Phase 3 · Instrumentation & Measurement

Timeline: Week 2–4

Purpose: Get real per-request cost data flowing into a queryable store.

Exit criteria: Instrumentation live in prod. One billing cycle reconciled within ±10%.

Primary artifacts:

  • Artifact 05 — Metering & Instrumentation Requirements (Technical)
  • Artifact 06 — Pilot Measurement Plan (Sampling + Calibration) (Technical + Finance)

Phase 4 · Modeling

Timeline: Week 3–5

Purpose: Build unit economics, componentized forecast, and scenarios.

Exit criteria: Cost/successful-outcome stated in one sentence. Forecast reconciles. Top 3 sensitivities identified.

Primary artifacts:

  • Artifact 07 — AI Unit Economics Calculator (Finance + Product)
  • Artifact 08 — AI Cost Forecast Model (Monthly, by Component) (Finance)
  • Artifact 09 — Scenario Planning & Sensitivity (Finance + Product)

Phase 5 · Optimization & Benefits

Timeline: Week 4–5

Purpose: Turn cost model into an action plan and honest attributable benefits.

Exit criteria: ≥ 6 quantified levers with owners. Attributable benefit signed by Business Owner.

Primary artifacts:

  • Artifact 10 — Optimization Options Catalog (Product + Technical)
  • Artifact 11 — Benefits Value Calculator (Incremental & Attributable) (Business + Finance)

Phase 6 · Business Case & Governance

Timeline: Week 5–6

Purpose: Deliver a decision-ready package for the executive committee.

Exit criteria: Exec committee decision recorded in the Artifact 1 Decision Log.

Primary artifacts:

  • Artifact 12 — ROI / NPV Model (Finance)
  • Artifact 13 — Executive One-Pager (Business Case) (Product + Business)
  • Artifact 14 — Full Business Case Narrative (Business + Consultant)
  • Artifact 15 — Risk Register & Controls Checklist (Risk/Compliance)
  • Artifact 16 — Evaluation & Quality Gate (Product + Risk)

Phase 7 · Operate & Steady State

Timeline: Ongoing

Purpose: Sustainable governance after the consultant leaves.

Exit criteria: First monthly close on time with the new operating model. Anomaly playbook rehearsed.

Primary artifacts:

  • Artifact 17 — Cost Management Operating Model (RACI + Cadence) (Business + Finance)
  • Artifact 18 — Chargeback / Showback Allocation (Finance)
  • Artifact 19 — Vendor & Pricing Negotiation Tracker (Finance + Procurement)
  • Artifact 20 — Data Dictionary (Technical)
  • Artifact 21 — Cost Anomaly Playbook (Platform + Finance)

Governance cadence

Once the operating model is live (from Artifact 17), the framework runs on a fixed cadence — daily anomaly checks, weekly unit-economics review, monthly close and reconciliation, quarterly portfolio review, annual framework refresh. See Governance Cadence for the full table.
