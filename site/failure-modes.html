Failure Modes

The ten most common ways enterprise AI cost management engagements fail — and how the 21-artifact pack forces the discipline that prevents each one.

1

Skipping intake

Every 'yes' commits weeks of work. Portfolio Intake (Phase 0) is the tier decision that determines how much of the pack applies. Skipping it means every project gets the T1 treatment — including ones that should have been T3 or declined outright.

Prevented in Phase 0 · Portfolio Intake

2

Pricing before drivers

Writing prices into Artifact 3 before Artifact 2 (workload decomposition) is complete. You end up with prices for the wrong things, and the model has no defensible unit basis.

Prevented in Phase 2 · Decomposition & Boundary

3

Instrumentation as an afterthought

Building the forecast first, then designing metering to match. The result: forecast components you cannot measure and measured signals you cannot reconcile. Artifact 5 must be co-designed with Artifact 4.

Prevented in Phase 3 · Instrumentation & Measurement

4

Reconciliation not enforced

Skipping the ±10% reconciliation gate at end of Phase 3. Every executive review after that point is on unaudited numbers, and any surprise invoice destroys the credibility of the model.

Prevented in Phase 3 · Instrumentation & Measurement

5

Cost per outcome missing

Reporting cost per request instead of cost per successful outcome. Retry rates, escalations, and rejects all inflate the true unit cost — sometimes 2–3x. If you cannot state cost per outcome in one sentence, Phase 4 is not done.

Prevented in Phase 4 · Modeling

6

Optimization without ownership

Artifact 10 (optimization catalog) with unowned rows is a wishlist, not a plan. Every quantified lever must have a named owner and a deadline before the exec committee sees the business case.

Prevented in Phase 5 · Optimization & Benefits

7

Benefits that were going to happen anyway

Counting benefits that would have happened without the AI investment. Artifact 11 exists precisely to keep the business case honest — attributable-only benefits with a signed baseline.

Prevented in Phase 5 · Optimization & Benefits

8

Business case as narrative, not decision

A 40-page narrative with no clear decision requested. Artifact 13 (Exec One-Pager) forces you to distill the ask. If it does not fit on one page, the committee will not read it.

Prevented in Phase 6 · Business Case & Governance

9

Consultant leaves before operating model

Ending the engagement at business-case approval instead of at first successful monthly close. Without Artifact 17 (Operating Model) actually running, the framework dies within a quarter.

Prevented in Phase 7 · Operate & Steady State

10

Anomaly playbook never rehearsed

Writing Artifact 21 and never running a simulation. The first real anomaly then finds it — and the response is slower and more expensive than it should be. Rehearse a simulated 30-percent cost spike before the consultant departs.

Prevented in Phase 7 · Operate & Steady State

Using this list

Review these ten modes at the end of every phase gate. If you cannot demonstrate that the current engagement is not exhibiting the ones relevant to your phase, do not proceed — fix the discipline first. This is the single highest-leverage practice in the framework.
